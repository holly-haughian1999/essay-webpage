The Beginnings of the World Wide Web

Firstly, before discussing the views of the Web, I want to discuss what it actually is. It began back in 1989, when Sir Tim-Berners Lee published “Information Management: A Proposal”. This paper brought together both hypertext and the Internet, creating the World Wide Web, a universal system made for sharing and disbursing information. (BBC, 2014) Tim included HTML, URL and HTTP in his proposal for making the web, all of which are still in use today in the world. He began to use a software called Enquire, which then led on to the idea of connecting society and its ideas and technology. The Web came together from ideas by many people,

“The Web arose as an answer to an open challenge, through the swirling together of influences, ideas, and realisations from many sides.”(Berners-Lee, T. 1999)

Sir Tim Berners-Lee was born on the 8th June 1955 in London. He completed his studies of Physics at Queen’s College in Oxford. While many think the World Wide Web is the internet, this is not the case. The internet is a collection of global networks, while the world wide web is a system used to access the information from the internet, it acts as a tool allowing us to use the internet.
